You are extracting game resolution mechanics from an RPG sourcebook.

## Your Task
Identify the core dice resolution system. Games use different methods:

1. **Dice Pool** (World of Darkness, Shadowrun): Roll multiple dice, count successes >= difficulty
2. **Sum Bands** (PbtA, Blades): Roll dice, sum total, compare to outcome bands (6-, 7-9, 10+)
3. **Target Number** (D&D, d20): Roll die + modifiers vs difficulty class

## Look For

### Resolution Method Indicators
- "roll X dice" or "dice pool" → dice_pool method
- "roll 2d6" with outcome ranges → sum_bands method
- "roll d20" or "difficulty class" or "DC" → target_number method

### Dice Pool Systems
- Die type (d6, d10, d20)
- Pool composition ("Attribute + Ability", "Stat + Skill")
- Success threshold ("each die showing X or higher")
- Default difficulty (usually 6-8)
- Difficulty range and named levels (Easy: 3, Standard: 6, Hard: 8)
- 1s canceling successes
- Botch rules (0 successes with 1s rolled)
- Outcome tiers by number of successes (1=marginal, 2-3=success, 4+=exceptional)

### Sum-Based Systems
- Dice rolled (2d6, 2d10)
- Outcome bands (6-: failure, 7-9: mixed, 10+: success)
- Modifiers added to roll (+2 Forward, +1 Ongoing)

### Common Features
- Willpower/resource spending for automatic successes
- Specialty bonuses (extra die, reroll)
- Automatic success rules (when dice aren't needed)
- Extended actions (accumulating successes)
- Contested/opposed rolls
